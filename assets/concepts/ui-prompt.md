# Ticker full-page UI concept prompt (for GPT-image / Nanobanana)
Run as one generation; also try cropping to single panels afterward.

---

UI/UX design mockup, three desktop app screens side by side on one wide canvas, labeled "1 START", "2 LIVE", "3 FULL TIME". High-fidelity product design, clean and minimal, Dribbble-quality.

THE PRODUCT: "TICKER" is a web toy: a Tamagotchi that lives and dies by a real World Cup match. The entire app is one photorealistic handheld device — a translucent ruby-red heart-shaped virtual pet console (like a 90s Game Boy accessory: glossy candy plastic, visible internal circuits, two round red physical buttons below the screen, small speaker grille). Inside its rectangular dark LCD screen lives a cute pixel-art heart creature wearing a team-colored football scarf, rendered in retro handheld style: dark phosphor screen, pale glowing green monochrome text, subtle scanlines, chunky pixel font (Press Start 2P style). A live football match feeds the creature's heartbeat: danger makes it panic, goals against make it flatline and crack, the fan's job is to CALM it and REVIVE it. The page around the device must feel like calm premium product photography — soft studio white background (#f2f2f4), the device centered with a soft contact shadow as if resting on a seamless white table, minimal typography, generous whitespace, nothing decorative competing with the device. All gameplay UI appears INSIDE the LCD; the page only carries quiet labels.

SCREEN 1 — START: Device centered. Above it, small pixel-font wordmark "TICKER" with tagline "your team plays. your heart takes the damage. don't let it break." The LCD shows a boot/start state: pixel TICKER logo, the heart creature waving, and a match picker list drawn in glowing green pixel text: "TONIGHT — SPAIN vs AUSTRIA 19:00 / PORTUGAL vs CROATIA 23:00", with one match highlighted by a blinking selector arrow, and below it "PICK YOUR SIDE: ◄ SPAIN ►" with the creature's scarf previewing that team's colors. Bottom of LCD blinks "PRESS A TO START". Below the device on the white page: a single restrained row of small rounded match chips (team names + kickoff time, one chip highlighted red), and in the top-right corner of the page a tiny quiet wallet pill "connected · devnet". Bottom edge: one thin caption line "A = SELECT · B = SWITCH".

SCREEN 2 — LIVE MATCH: Same device, same white page. LCD now mid-game: top row pixel text "ESP 1-0 AUT   67'", a live green EKG heartbeat line scrolling across the middle, the pixel heart creature standing on a dashed pixel ground line looking panicked with sweat drops, "142 BPM" bottom left, state word "PANIC!!" bottom right, narration line "they're building…" and a blinking prompt "A · CALM IT DOWN". The page around the device is unchanged except the far page edges glow faintly warm red (subtle vignette, danger ambience) and the left physical button has a soft red glow halo. Everything else identical and calm — the contrast between calm page and panicking screen is the design story.

SCREEN 3 — FULL TIME / REPORT CARD: Device centered, LCD shows "FULL TIME · ESP 2-1 AUT" and a tiny sleeping creature with a stitched scar. Below the device on the white page floats ONE new element: a shareable report card — a small clean white card with subtle shadow, styled like a collectible: pixel heart portrait with scar, the full match's EKG line as a jagged green graph strip, score "ESP 2-1 AUT", stats row "</3 x2 · revived x1 · survived", a 7-day streak of tiny heart icons, and a small "SHARE" pixel button. Card typography mixes pixel font headings with clean sans-serif details. Under it a faint caption "your match, your heartbeat".

STYLE RULES: photorealistic device + pixel-art screen contents + minimal Swiss-style page layout. Palette: studio white page, ruby red translucent plastic, phosphor green LCD glow, single red accent. NO clutter, NO dashboards, NO extra panels, NO fake browser chrome, NO watermarks, NO lorem ipsum walls. Text short and legible. The three screens must look like the same product at three moments, identical device position and lighting across all three.
